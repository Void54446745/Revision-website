Context

The Industrial Revolution refers to the period of major technological change where machine manufacturing in factories replaced rural work

The resulting movement of rural workers to urban areas of England caused huge overpopulation in major cities, which in turn led to:

  • Fewer jobs and lower pay
  • Lack of housing and inhumane living conditions for the poor
  • Poor sanitation and hygiene led to the spread of disease
  • Higher crime rates

Workhouses (aka ‘The Poor House’) was where many poverty-stricken Victorians were sent if they could not support themselves or their families.

Workhouses offered a place to live, but inmates were forced to work without pay, and suffered inhumane and dangerous living and working conditions.

The 1834 ‘Poor Law’ criminalised the poor, by forcing them into the workhouses if they could not support themselves.

Charles Dickens, who visited the workhouses, condemned them for being cruel, inhumane, and a misguided solution to poverty. He used his novels and journalism to protest the Poor Law.

Minor Characters

IDEA 1: Dickens uses his minor characters to expose the importance of being socially responsible; generosity, benevolence and love bring greater happiness than greed or avarice.
Dickens uses Belle’s rejection of Scrooge to expose the personal consequences of greed: Scrooge’s materialism and selfishness causes him to lose love and family. “Another idol has displaced me… a golden one… I have seen your nobler aspirations fall off one by one, until the master-passion, Gain, engrosses you” Noun (‘idol’) → comparing money to something one might worship like a God. Emphasises Scrooge’s sinful greed as he prioritises material wealth over his loved ones. Personification (‘master-passion, Gain’) → emphasises the power and influence that greed has over Scrooge. Verb (‘engrosses’) → literally, describes something overwhelming/overcoming a person. Metaphor → highlights how Scrooge is completely obsessed with the thought of gaining wealth, which distracts him from what is important in life (love, family etc.) 2. Dickens constructs Fezziwig as the model Victorian employer: his kindness and generosity towards his employees is the antithesis of Scrooge’s callous treatment of Bob Cratchit. The happiness he gives, is quite as great as if it cost a fortune.” Dickens constructs Fezziwig as the model Victorian employer, as he treats his workers with kindness and generosity, and prioritises their happiness over his own financial gain. Simile comparing “happiness” to “fortune” = key turning point in Scrooge’s characterisation. He now recognises the joy that generosity and kindness can bring. Scrooge now recognises that material wealth does not bring you happiness. L5 AO2: Dickens purposefully crafts Fezziwig as a foil to Scrooge: his kind and generous treatment of his workers is the direct antithesis of Scrooge’s selfish treatment of Bob Cratchit. 3. Dickens uses Ignorance and Want as a symbol of far-reaching impacts of social negligence in Victorian society: the young children suffer extreme starvation, disease and a loss of morality due to their poverty. “Yellow, meagre, ragged, wolfish… where angels should have sat enthroned, devils lurked” List of adjectives → highlights the vast scale of the children’s suffering due to their poverty. Not only emotional impacts of suffering, but very clear physical impacts as a result of starvation, disease etc. “Yellow” and “meagre” (thin) → reflect the children’s malnourishment due to their lack of basic necessities. Direct impact of overpopulation in London due to the Industrial Revolution. Animalistic imagery “wolfish” → exposes how poverty has a dehumanising effect on the children (forced to live in inhumane conditions etc.) Juxtaposition “angels” vs. “devils” → exposes how poverty causes the young children to lose their innocence and morality, perhaps because they are forced to turn to crime.
IDEA 2 Dickens uses his minor characters to expose the importance of maintaining close and loving relationships with friends and family, rather than living in isolation.
1.Dickens constructs Fred as a foil to Scrooge: his unconditional love and kindness towards his family not only brings happiness, but has a transformational impact on Scrooge. “Begged like a boy to stay” “Begged” (verb) → emphasises Scrooge’s desperation to continue experiencing the happiness and togetherness that Fred’s Christmas party bring. Simile “like a boy” → highlights Scrooge’s childish excitement: he now recognises the joy that celebrating Christmas with family can bring. CONTRAST: Scrooge’s dismissive attitude towards Christmas at the start of the novel (views it as a time for making “idle people merry”) Foreshadows → Scrooge’s metaphorical “rebirth” at the end of the novel, as he exclaims “I’m quite a baby!”.

Scrooge.

BEGINNING = Scrooge views the poor as worthless and undeserving of support “If they’d rather die, they had better do it and decrease the surplus population”. Conditional tense (if they… they had) → emphasises Scrooge’s callousness towards the working class: he views death as a choice for the poor, and therefore does see the point in generosity/social responsibility. Numerical jargon ‘decrease’ and ‘surplus’ → exposes Scrooge’s dehumanisation of the Victorian poor: he views them as a homogenous group, rather than as individuals with emotions. GRADE 9 CONTEXT LINK: Dickens crafts Scrooge as an embodiment of the economic principles of Thomas Malthus, who blamed the poor’s immorality for the overpopulation of Britain’s major cities. L5 AO2: Dickens purposefully crafts Fezziwig as a foil to Scrooge: his kind and generous treatment of his workers is the direct antithesis of Scrooge’s selfish treatment of Bob Cratchit. “I should like to say a word or two to my clerk just now” Tone of regret → emphasise a clear turning point in Dickens’ characterisation of Scrooge: he now recognises how cold-heartedly and selfishly he has treated Bob Cratchit and feels ashamed as a result. Foreshadows → Scrooge’s generosity towards Bob Cratchit at the end of the novel, when he raises his salary and orders him to “make up the fires!”. “Spirit!”, said Scrooge, with an interest he had never felt before, ‘tell me if Tiny Tim will live”. Exclamation + imperative (‘tell me’) → exposes Scrooge’s desperation to learn Tiny Tim’s fate. Also highlights his transformed attitude towards the poor: he now views the working class as individuals with feelings, and cares about their suffering. Phrase ‘interest he had never felt before’ → further amplifies Scrooge’s change. At the beginning, he dismissed the poor’s suffering (‘if they’d rather die, they’d better do it and decrease the surplus population) “He started back appalled…He tried to say they were fine children, but the words choked themselves” Adjective ‘appalled’→ exposes Scrooge’s shock and horror at the children’s malnourishment and dehumanisation. Also exposes Scrooge’s profound ignorance of the reality faced by the Victorian poor: starvation, disease, moral corruption. ‘Fine children’ vs. ‘choked’ → Scrooge attempts to ignore the children’s condition but is unable to. ‘Choked’ (associated with pain/struggle) → exposes Scrooge’s painful realisation of both his own ignorance and the harsh reality of life for the working class. Represents Dickens’ message that the Victorian upper class need to confront the horrifying consequences of their own greed, and treat the Victorian poor with generosity in order to improve their quality of life. END = SCROOGE’S TRANSFORMATION: EMBRACES THE POOR Scrooge donates to the charity workers: “A great many back payments are included in it” Charity Workers: “Lord bless us!” Adjectives → expose Scrooge’s extensive generosity, and his willingness to make amends for his previous selfishness. Also - reveals the way he embraces Christian principles of generosity. Religious language → emphasises the charity workers’ gratitude. Also exposes the transformational impact that one person’s generosity can have on so many people. Scrooge becomes a “second father to Tiny Tim” // “Golden sunlight” “heavenly sky” Symbolism “second father”→ exposes how, due to his transformed attitude towards the poor, Scrooge is now a model citizen in his society, who gives care and support to the vulnerable. Pathetic fallacy → Dickens purposefully creates a contrast between the weather at the beginning (‘cold, bleak, biting weather’) and the end to expose the way one selfish man’s transformation can have a positive and healing impact on an entire society.
